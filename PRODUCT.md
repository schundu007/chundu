# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, recruiter-first. Senior recruiters and engineering hiring managers arrive with a question: *does this candidate clear the bar?* They scan for titles, tenure, and impact signals in under 30 seconds. Technical peers — staff/principal engineers, conference organizers, open source collaborators — arrive second and read deeper, looking for evidence of craft and systems depth.

Primary job: give the recruiter a fast, confident yes. Secondary job: give the technical reviewer something to respect.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Sudhakar Chundu, a Staff DevOps Engineer / SRE with 18+ years in GPU infrastructure, FinOps, and platform engineering. The site exists to land FAANG-level senior IC and staff roles. Success looks like a recruiter forwarding the profile and a panel arriving already convinced of the caliber.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, systems-thinking. Not loud, not corporate-glossy. The portfolio should feel like a well-engineered system: everything in its place, nothing wasted, the complexity handled invisibly so the expertise speaks cleanly.

## Anti-references

- Generic Bootstrap/template developer portfolios: same hero + skills grid + contact form, endlessly repeated. No personality, no authority.
- Overloaded agency-portfolio style: particle explosions, scroll-jacking, design-school flair that buries the actual work.
- Dark SaaS clichés: neon purple gradients, hero metric cards with glowing borders, glassmorphism as decoration.

## Design Principles

1. **Signal through structure.** The layout itself communicates systems thinking. Hierarchy, white space, and typographic rhythm tell the story before the copy does.
2. **Recruiters pass, engineers close.** The first-pass read must answer the recruiter's question in seconds. Depth and evidence close the technical panel.
3. **Evidence over adjectives.** Every claim earns its place with a number, an outcome, or a named system. No filler words.
4. **Calm authority.** Energy through precision, not noise. Accent and motion guide attention; they don't create it.
5. **The medium mirrors the work.** An SRE's portfolio should feel like an SRE designed it: reliable, maintainable, no surprises.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. High contrast between the near-black base and text/accent layers. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for the neural network background and any scroll animations. Color is never the sole carrier of meaning.
