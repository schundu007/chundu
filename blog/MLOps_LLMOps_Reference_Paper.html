Unifying Classic Machine Learning and Generative AI Under One Governance Plane

A Reference Architecture and Operational Practices for Industrial and Manufacturing AI

Sudhakar Chundu IEEE · chundubabu@gmail.com
June 2026
~45 min read
8 Sections
Download Paper (.docx)

Abstract

Manufacturing and other industrial organizations are now running two kinds of AI side by side: the familiar predictive stack — yield models, vision-based defect detectors, failure forecasts — trained on equipment and process data, and a generative layer of copilots and agents that work over engineering documents. The two are usually built by separate teams on separate tooling, and that split is where governance, security, and delivery begin to fray.


This paper presents a vendor-neutral reference architecture that places both lifecycles under a single governance plane, adapted for the strict networking, edge-deployment, and asynchronous document-ingestion realities of industrial environments, together with operational practices for taking models and language-based systems from prototype to monitored production. We define a drift taxonomy specific to manufacturing execution data and distinguish detection that is possible without labels from detection that requires delayed ground truth. We contrast retrieval-augmented generation, direct generation, and agentic patterns, and give selection guidance. Finally, we define an evaluation methodology that corrects for model-graded evaluator bias and a continuous-delivery pipeline that gates generative systems with the same rigor applied to predictive models.

Key Contributions

  • A vendor-neutral reference architecture spanning a cloud control plane and the industrial edge, including an asynchronous document-ingestion and vectorization pipeline.
  • A baselining methodology for predictive models and foundation-model selection, with metric selection guidance for imbalanced manufacturing datasets.
  • A drift taxonomy for manufacturing execution data that separates label-free from label-dependent detection — covariate drift, concept drift, prediction drift, and pipeline drift.
  • Selection guidance across retrieval-augmented generation, direct generation, and agentic patterns with decision criteria grounded in latency, grounding requirements, and cost.
  • An evaluation methodology that corrects for model-graded evaluator bias using adversarial calibration and human-anchored rubrics.
  • Continuous-delivery and edge-aware monitoring patterns that gate generative systems with the same rigor applied to predictive models, including signed container delivery to constrained edge hardware.
